## 2. Problem Summary

AIEOS requires users to manually navigate 6 kits containing 33+ artifact types, understand artifact dependencies, sequence AI sessions correctly, and track artifact state without tooling support. This creates a high barrier to entry that prevents non-expert users from operating the framework independently and makes experienced users slow and error-prone.

Source: PFD-CONSOLE-001

## 3. Target Users

| PFD Reference | User Group | Relevance to Hypotheses |
|---------------|-----------|------------------------|
| UG-1 | Engineers and Architects | Primary operators of AIEOS — most affected by manual sequencing, state tracking, and session setup overhead |
| UG-2 | Product Managers | Locked out of self-service usage — key beneficiaries of a guided interface that does not require framework expertise |
| UG-3 | Team/Org Leaders Evaluating Adoption | Cannot assess AIEOS without deep framework knowledge — beneficiaries of reduced barrier to evaluation |

## 4. Value Hypotheses

### HYP-1: Guided Sequencing Reduces Errors and Time

**We believe that** providing guided artifact sequencing with automated dependency checking **for** UG-1 (Engineers and Architects) **will achieve** a reduction in sequencing errors and time spent navigating between kit files.

- **Belief:** Engineers currently spend significant effort determining the next artifact, locating the right files, and verifying upstream artifacts are frozen. Automating this sequencing will eliminate navigation overhead and prevent out-of-order generation.
- **Target users:** UG-1
- **Expected outcome:** Users complete an artifact generation cycle without sequencing errors and with measurably less time spent on process navigation (as opposed to artifact content work).
- **Evidence criteria:** Users generate artifacts in the correct order without manual playbook consultation; no instances of generating artifacts against unfrozen upstream inputs.
- **Falsification criteria:** If more than 25% of users still consult kit playbook files directly to determine the next step after using the guided interface for 3 or more artifact cycles, the hypothesis is false.

### HYP-2: Non-Technical Users Can Operate Independently

**We believe that** providing a guided interface that abstracts file-system navigation and AI session setup **for** UG-2 (Product Managers) **will achieve** independent operation of PIK discovery processes without requiring a technical guide.

- **Belief:** Product managers are currently excluded from self-service usage because the process requires navigating markdown file structures and setting up AI sessions with specific inputs. A guided interface that presents the process as a series of steps with clear inputs and outputs will enable independent participation.
- **Target users:** UG-2
- **Expected outcome:** Product managers can complete a Discovery Intake, review a PFD, and approve a freeze without assistance from a technical user.
- **Evidence criteria:** A product manager with no prior AIEOS experience completes a PIK flow (intake through PFD freeze) without intervention from someone with framework expertise.
- **Falsification criteria:** If fewer than 50% of non-technical test users can complete a Discovery Intake and review a generated PFD without asking for help within 60 minutes of first use, the hypothesis is false.

### HYP-3: Integrated Validation Improves Artifact Quality

**We believe that** integrating validator execution directly into the artifact generation workflow **for** UG-1 and UG-2 **will achieve** higher first-pass validation rates and fewer rework cycles.

- **Belief:** Currently, validation is a separate manual step that users may skip or forget. Integrating validation into the flow ensures every artifact is checked before freeze, and surfacing PASS/FAIL results immediately gives users a chance to address issues before moving on.
- **Target users:** UG-1, UG-2
- **Expected outcome:** Artifacts reach PASS status with fewer generation-validation-rework cycles compared to manual operation.
- **Evidence criteria:** Average number of rework cycles per artifact decreases; no artifacts are frozen without passing validation.
- **Falsification criteria:** If the average number of validation-rework cycles per artifact is not lower than 3 (the estimated manual baseline) after 5 completed initiatives, the hypothesis is false.

### HYP-4: Reduced Evaluation Barrier Enables Adoption Decisions

**We believe that** providing a visible, walkable AIEOS process **for** UG-3 (Team/Org Leaders) **will achieve** faster and more confident adoption decisions.

- **Belief:** Evaluating AIEOS currently requires reading extensive documentation and mentally simulating artifact flows. A guided interface that demonstrates the process in action will make the framework tangible and reduce the evaluation effort required to make an adoption decision.
- **Target users:** UG-3
- **Expected outcome:** Evaluators can form an informed opinion about AIEOS fit within a single guided session rather than requiring days of documentation review.
- **Evidence criteria:** An evaluator with no prior AIEOS knowledge can describe what AIEOS does and how it works after completing one guided walkthrough.
- **Falsification criteria:** If fewer than 60% of evaluators can accurately describe the AIEOS artifact flow and its value proposition after a single guided session of 90 minutes or less, the hypothesis is false.

## 5. Hypothesis Prioritization

| Rank | Hypothesis | Expected Impact | Confidence Level | Rationale |

| 1 | HYP-1 | High | High | Directly addresses PP-1 and PP-2 (knowledge prerequisite and manual sequencing); affects all users on every interaction; evidence from direct experience is strong |
| 2 | HYP-3 | High | Medium | Directly addresses PP-2 (manual sequencing includes skipped validation); high impact on artifact quality but confidence is medium because the rework baseline is estimated, not measured |
| 3 | HYP-2 | High | Medium | Directly addresses PP-4 (non-technical user exclusion); high strategic impact for adoption but confidence is medium because no non-technical user has attempted AIEOS |
| 4 | HYP-4 | Medium | Low | Addresses the adoption evaluation barrier (UG-3); impact depends on whether evaluation difficulty is actually the primary adoption blocker; low confidence because no evaluators have been observed |

**Prioritization basis:** Impact assessed by number of pain points addressed and frequency of the affected interaction. Confidence assessed by directness of supporting evidence (Known > Believed > Assumed).

## 6. Success Metrics

**SM-1: Process Navigation Time**
- **Hypothesis:** HYP-1
- **Metric:** Percentage of artifact generation time spent on process navigation (locating files, determining next step, verifying dependencies) vs. content work (reviewing inputs, editing artifacts, making decisions)
- **Target:** Process navigation consumes less than 20% of total artifact generation time
- **Measurement method:** User self-report or observation during artifact generation sessions; compare against manual baseline

**SM-2: Non-Technical Completion Rate**
- **Hypothesis:** HYP-2
- **Metric:** Percentage of non-technical users who complete a PIK discovery flow (intake through PFD review) without assistance
- **Target:** ≥ 50% of non-technical test users complete unassisted within 60 minutes
- **Measurement method:** Observed task completion during usability testing with product manager participants

**SM-3: First-Pass Validation Rate**
- **Hypothesis:** HYP-3
- **Metric:** Average number of generation-validation-rework cycles per artifact before achieving PASS
- **Target:** Average ≤ 2 cycles per artifact (estimated manual baseline: 3)
- **Measurement method:** Count of validation runs per artifact as recorded by the console

**SM-4: Evaluator Comprehension**
- **Hypothesis:** HYP-4
- **Metric:** Percentage of evaluators who can accurately describe the AIEOS artifact flow after one guided session
- **Target:** ≥ 60% of evaluators
- **Measurement method:** Post-session structured interview or questionnaire

## 7. Falsification Criteria

| Hypothesis | Falsification Condition | Evidence Required |
|-----------|------------------------|-------------------|
| HYP-1 | More than 25% of users still consult kit playbook files directly after 3+ artifact cycles using the guided interface | Usage observation or self-report across at least 5 users |
| HYP-2 | Fewer than 50% of non-technical test users complete Discovery Intake + PFD review without help within 60 minutes | Observed task completion with at least 4 non-technical participants |
| HYP-3 | Average validation-rework cycles per artifact is not lower than 3 after 5 completed initiatives | Validation cycle counts from console logs across 5 initiatives |
| HYP-4 | Fewer than 60% of evaluators can accurately describe AIEOS artifact flow after a single guided session of ≤ 90 minutes | Post-session interview results with at least 5 evaluators |

## 8. Dependencies and Risks

### Hypothesis Dependencies
- HYP-2 depends on HYP-1: non-technical users can only operate independently if the guided sequencing works correctly for all users first
- HYP-4 depends on HYP-1 and HYP-2: the evaluation experience is only compelling if both technical and non-technical flows work

### Hypothesis Conflicts
- No conflicts identified between hypotheses

### Risks to Validity
- All falsification thresholds are based on estimated baselines (manual operation), not measured baselines — actual manual performance may differ from estimates
- Small sample sizes during initial testing may not be statistically significant
- HYP-2 and HYP-4 involve user groups (PMs, evaluators) who have not yet interacted with AIEOS in any form — their reactions may differ from expectations
